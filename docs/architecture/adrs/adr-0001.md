# ADR-0001: Knowledge Model as Canonical Source of Truth

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-07-21
**Deciders:** Core maintainers

## Context

The system must decide what serves as the authoritative source of truth for all knowledge. Options include: the storage layer, the search index, the AI model, or an independent canonical model.

Current knowledge management systems conflate storage with meaning. A database stores records but does not understand entities. A search index retrieves text but does not understand relationships. An AI model generates outputs but does not own the knowledge model.

## Decision

The canonical knowledge model is the source of truth. The knowledge model represents reality independently of storage technology. Every entity, relationship, component, artifact, and capability is represented within the canonical model. All other representations are derived.

## Alternatives Considered

### Option 1: Database as source of truth
- Pros: Simple, familiar, transactional
- Cons: Ties the knowledge model to a specific storage technology. Replacing the database requires rewriting the domain model.
- Why not chosen: Violates storage independence.

### Option 2: Search index as source of truth
- Pros: Fast retrieval, full-text search
- Cons: Search indexes are optimized for retrieval, not for canonical representation. They lose metadata, relationships, and versioning.
- Why not chosen: Search indexes are derived data. They cannot be the source of truth.

### Option 3: AI model as source of truth
- Pros: Intelligent, adaptive
- Cons: AI outputs are probabilistic, not deterministic. AI models change over time. AI outputs are not auditable.
- Why not chosen: AI is a component, not the system. AI outputs are derived data.

## Consequences

### Positive
- The knowledge model is storage-independent. Any storage engine may be replaced without changing the domain model.
- The knowledge model is auditable. Every change is versioned and traceable.
- The knowledge model is durable. It outlives any individual technology choice.

### Negative
- The canonical model requires its own persistence layer, adding complexity.
- Derived data must be rebuilt when the canonical model changes, requiring event-driven infrastructure.

### Risks
- If the canonical model is not well-designed, it may not capture all necessary knowledge representations. Mitigation: the model is extensible through components.
